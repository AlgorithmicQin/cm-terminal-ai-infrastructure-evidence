# Limitations

This package is intentionally narrow. It supports a reproducible evidence supplement for one latest-year issuer-level comparison and should not be read beyond that scope.

## Analytical Limits

- No segment-level attribution.
- No pure AI revenue estimate.
- No valuation.
- No target prices.
- No stock recommendations.
- No investment ranking.
- No expected return estimate.

## Comparability Limits

Cross-company comparability is limited because the companies occupy different economic layers: compute platforms, fabless challengers, foundry manufacturing, equipment, memory, networking, and facility infrastructure.

Fiscal years and period ends differ. Reporting currencies differ. No FX conversion is applied. Capital expenditure line items differ by issuer and are treated as documented company-level proxies.

Ratio comparison is directional and descriptive; it is not a fully normalized cross-company accounting model. The chart is not a ranking of company quality, investment attractiveness, or future performance.

## Company-Specific Limits

- Broadcom FY2024 includes VMware acquisition and infrastructure software effects, so consolidated metrics are not a clean AI semiconductor read.
- Micron is exposed to memory pricing, inventory, and capital cycles; AI/HBM relevance does not remove memory-cycle economics.
- Arista and Vertiv are downstream demand-transmission nodes. Their issuer-level metrics should not be read as compute-platform, foundry, memory, or semiconductor-equipment economics.
- TSMC and ASML have indirect AI infrastructure exposure through advanced manufacturing, foundry demand, lithography, customer CapEx, and non-AI end markets.

## Non-Advisory Limit

This package is descriptive and non-advisory. It does not recommend buying, selling, or holding any security.
